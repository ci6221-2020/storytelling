First confirmed case in Singapore

23 Jan 2020

Singapore's sees its first confirmed case of coronavirus, a 66-year-old man from Wuhan.

He flew from Guangzhou via China Southern flight CZ351 and arrived in Singapore with his family on Jan 20.

First Local Cluster in Singapore

4 Feb 2020

Singapore sees its first case of infection in the community at Yong Thai Hung, a Chinese health products shop in Lavender.

Two women are employees, the third is the maid of the women, and the fourth is a tour guide who had taken groups to the same shop.

DORSCON alert was raised: From yellow to orange

8 Feb 2020

Under the Disease Outbreak Response System Condition or Dorscon, orange means the outbreak is deemed to have moderate to high public health impact.

9 Feb 2020

Each customer is allowed to buy a maximum of:

'COVID-19' is named by the WHO

11 Feb 2020

The UN health agency announced that "COVID-19" will be the official name of the deadly coronavirus from China.

WHO chief Tedros explained that the name was chosen to avoid references to a specific geographical location, animal species or group of people.


First successful serological test to uncover a COVID-19 patient

25 Feb 2020

A research team at Duke-NUS Medical School has found a link between 2 COVID-19 clusters in Singpore via serological testing on 2 cases.

A couple, cases 83 and 91, are linked to the Life Church and Missions Singapore and came into contact with cases 8 and 9, a couple from Wuhan.

A huge cluster at SAFRA Jurong was discovered

5 Mar 2020

Since the cluster was discovered, MOH found that many of the cases were transmitted during social activities and gatherings among seniors.

This cluster was related to a function, attended by members of a Hokkien singing group, in the ballroom of Joy Garden Restautant at SAFRA Jurong.

First two deaths in Singapore

21 Mar 2020

A 75-year-old Singaporean woman and a 64-year-old Indonesian have died from COVID-19.

Both of them had underlying health conditions and they were in ICU.
